Problem

Kid City was an innovative new childcare center faced with the challenge of differentiating itself from a flooded childcare market, building trust with parents, and still maintaining a sense of fun for the children--all at the same time. Compared to other childcare centers, it had a higher startup cost alongside the normal startup challenges, so we had to make an impression and ensure growth at a fast pace.

Challenges

In a market where there were hundreds of competing childcare centers, Kid City needed to stand out. Additionally, the business plan called for positioning the company has a high-end solution for childcare. So we were faced with the question: how do we maintain a sense of fun, appeal to high-end customers, and stand out from every other childcare center in the market?

Solution

In order to solve these problems, I designed a brand and marketing that looked and felt like a "big box" chain. This would build trust by giving parents the impression that KC was large and wasn't just a "mom and pop" daycare. This involved a carefully design logo, city-themed and cohesive marketing and design work, numerous high-quality illustrations (many of them custom), a high-quality commercial, and much more. As a result of these efforts, Kid City grew to over 500 members in less than a year, moved to a new space with more than twice the square footage to keep up with the growth, started a waiting list, and had parents regularly commenting on the design and asking if KC was a franchise they could buy into. It was a monumental success.
