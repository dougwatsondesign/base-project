The Ethos Project

Problem

Ethos Project oversaw programs that helped people get into technology careers, and they needed branding and materials that walked the line between "technology-oriented" and "accessible". As a nonprofit that would be working with top leaders in the city, they needed a clean, professional brand that would stand out from other organizations in the community.

Challenges

Ethos Project conducted business with prestigious business leaders, politicians, and nonprofit leaderhip, but it also conducted programs for low-income areas. The challenge was developing a brand that would appeal to high-end thought leaders while remaining accessible to the average person. Additionally, Ethos Project's programs often required realying complex information in a way that people could easily follow.

Solution

The design developed maintained the "feel" of a tech company, but also used fun splashes of color that would appeal to a wider market and make program participants feel that technology careers were accessible to them. The resulting designs conveyed feelings of fun, foward-thinking, encouragement, and importance. Both leaders and program participants regularly commented positively on the design work, and partners loved the easy-to-follow infographics and graphics work. The organization's years-long struggle with accurately conveying what they did was over: now they had a clear brand that accomplished exactly what it was supposed to.
